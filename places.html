Explore Karnataka's Heritage

1. Hampi: Ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the capital of the Vijaynagara Empire and teems with remains from a time long gone. It is an archaeological delight, famous for monuments like the Stone Chariot and the Virupaksha Temple. Don't forget to try bouldering when you're here.

2. Bengaluru: The Garden and Silicon City

Bengaluru is a fascinating, fast-paced amalgam of the past and the future. It stands as India's Silicon City, yet remains deeply rooted in history with landmarks like the majestic Vidhana Soudha, the seat of the state legislature. The city balances this modernity with serene green spaces such as the beautiful Lalbagh and Cubbon Park, offering a seamless blend of historical sites and bustling city life.

3. Coastal Karnataka: Temples and Beaches

The western coast of Karnataka is a mesmerizing blend of deep spirituality and pristine natural beauty. Journey to Murudeshwar, where the towering, ornate Raja Gopura stands beside the magnificent, second-tallest Shiva statue in the world, overlooking the Arabian Sea. Alternatively, find serenity and quiet introspection on the less crowded shores, such as the famous crescent-shaped Om Beach in Gokarna.

4. Jog Falls: The Majestic Natural Wonder

Jog Falls, created by the Sharavathi River in the Shivamogga district, is the second-highest plunge waterfall in India. Dropping from a height of 830 feet, the falls are a spectacular sight, especially during the monsoon season when the four distinct cascades—Raja, Roarer, Rocket, and Rani—merge into a powerful torrent, showcasing the raw, untamed natural beauty of the Western Ghats.
